VI. Stage 06 · Rollout & funding

From proposal
to power on Country.

Five stages move the system from approved concept to safely decommissioned infrastructure — each one designed around community consent, local capability and long-term care. Below the timeline, the funding pathway shows how the rollout is paid for.

01 Implementation Timeline

Five stages, one partnership.

Each stage has clear objectives, activities and deliverables. Together, they form a careful sequence — from initial consultation with Lama Lama stakeholders all the way through to end-of-life decommissioning, with community ownership built in at every step.

01
Stage 01

Consultation

Engineers Without Borders, 2026

Objectives
  • Identify and validate functional and non-functional requirements.
  • Achieve formal stakeholder approval.
  • Define installation constraints and environmental conditions.
Activities
  • Engage with Lama Lama stakeholders (Rangers, Elders, Yintinjigga Aboriginal Corporation).
  • Demonstrate a functional prototype.
  • Pursue a Ranger-based survey to gauge system acceptance.
Deliverables
  • Sign-off on implementation approval (Council + Rangers).
  • Signed agreement of agreed-upon deliverables and outcomes.
  • Consolidated stakeholder feedback and design changes.
02
Stage 02

System Procurement

Indigenous, 2022

Objectives
  • Translate approved requirements into a fully functional, tested system.
  • Provide clear, usable documentation for non-technical users (Rangers).
Activities
  • Finalise system design specifications.
  • Select contractors and suppliers.
  • Prepare documentation (manuals, guides, troubleshooting).
  • Package and transport system to site.
Deliverables
  • Fully built and tested system.
  • Verified performance test results.
  • Complete documentation (operator & maintenance).
03
Stage 03

Installation

Indigenous Desert Alliance, 2025

Objectives
  • Deploy an operational system without disrupting existing infrastructure.
  • Transfer operational knowledge to Rangers.
Activities
  • Transport technician to site.
  • Install system with non-invasive clamp sensors.
  • Perform system commissioning (power-on and signal testing).
  • Train Rangers in operation, maintenance and troubleshooting.
Deliverables
  • Installed and operational monitoring system.
  • Successful commissioning and validation results.
  • Baseline performance dataset.
04
Stage 04

Post-Implementation

Indigenous Desert Alliance, 2025

Objectives
  • Validate system performance under real-world conditions.
  • Identify and resolve early-stage faults or usability issues.
  • Interpret data to inform future scaling or redesign decisions.
Activities
  • Conduct regular check-ins (remote or in-person).
  • Monitor system performance and environmental durability.
  • Track key metrics (fault detection, downtime, alerts).
  • Analyse data against baseline performance.
Deliverables
  • Short-term performance report (0–6 months).
  • End-of-year performance report (12 months).
  • Second annual performance report (24 months).
  • Evidence of system impact (e.g. reduced downtime, improved detection).
05
Stage 05

End-of-Life & Maintenance

RS Group, 2021

Objectives
  • Enable local self-sufficiency rather than external repair reliance.
  • Minimise system downtime.
  • Safely decommission the system without damaging infrastructure.
Activities
  • Conduct periodic technical servicing.
  • Update firmware and calibrate sensors.
  • Manage system removal, recycling or upgrade at end-of-life.
Deliverables
  • Ongoing system functionality and reliability.
  • Maintenance records and logs.
  • Updated system documentation and training materials.
  • Safe system decommissioning, disposal/reuse plan.
Supporting evidence

Costings, prototypes & references.
