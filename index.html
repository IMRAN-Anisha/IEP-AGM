01 Executive Summary

A practical answer to an
invisible problem.

This website presents Team 7's design proposal for the Engineers Without Borders (EWB) Challenge Research Area 3.4: Energy Monitoring and Fault Diagnosis. The project focuses on supporting the Lama Lama community at Port Stewart, Queensland, where off-grid solar energy systems power essential ranger facilities and community services.

The community's energy infrastructure is vulnerable to faults caused by the region's tropical climate — including extreme heat, wet-season isolation, and wildlife interference. Because these systems operate in remote locations without continuous supervision, faults often go undetected until they cause major disruptions. When energy failures occur, they affect critical needs such as food storage, medical equipment, education, and ranger operations. Through our research and analysis, current fault diagnosis systems in the community are ambiguous and non-user-friendly, causing misdiagnosis and evidencing a clear gap in sufficient and effective energy fault diagnosis systems.

To address this challenge, our team proposes a low-cost, durable, and easy-to-use energy monitoring system that enables early fault detection without requiring specialist technical training. The solution aims to improve energy reliability, reduce downtime, and support the long-term sustainability of ranger activities and community wellbeing across Lama Lama Country.

This site provides a high-level overview of our proposed design, aimed to inform stakeholders including Lama Lama representatives and marketers to achieve implementation of the design. It includes evidence of decision-making processes, prototype testing, implementation plan and costing, as well as a detailed final design breakdown.

03 Why this matters

Energy reliability is community reliability.

Ranger operations

Rangers monitor and care for Country every day — from environmental sampling to fire management. Reliable power is what keeps their radios, GPS units and field equipment running.

Essential services

Solar power supports the things people can't go without: medical equipment, food refrigeration, and access to communication. A single unplanned outage can ripple across the community for days.

Remote isolation

During the wet season, road access is cut off for months at a time. When systems fail, help is hours or days away — making early detection and local repair the difference between disruption and disaster.

05 The team behind the work

Meet "A Good Mix"

A multidisciplinary student team from the Faculty of Engineering, working together through the Engineers Without Borders (EWB) Challenge to develop a human-centred response to Research Area 3.4.
