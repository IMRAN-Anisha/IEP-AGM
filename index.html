01 Executive Summary

A practical answer to an
invisible problem.

The project

This website presents Team 7's design proposal for the Engineers Without Borders (EWB) Challenge Research Area 3.4: Energy Monitoring and Fault Diagnosis. The project focuses on supporting the Lama Lama community at Port Stewart, Queensland, where off-grid solar energy systems power essential ranger facilities and community services.

The challenge

The community's energy infrastructure is vulnerable to faults caused by the region's tropical climate — including extreme heat, wet-season isolation, and wildlife interference. Because these systems operate in remote locations without continuous supervision, faults often go undetected until they cause major disruptions, affecting food storage, medical equipment, education, and ranger operations.

Through our research and analysis, current fault diagnosis systems in the community are ambiguous and non-user-friendly, causing misdiagnosis and evidencing a clear gap in sufficient and effective energy fault diagnosis.

Our solution

To address this challenge, our team proposes a low-cost, durable, and easy-to-use energy monitoring system that enables early fault detection without requiring specialist technical training. The solution aims to improve energy reliability, reduce downtime, and support the long-term sustainability of ranger activities and community wellbeing across Lama Lama Country.

This website

This site provides a high-level overview of our proposed design, aimed at stakeholders including Lama Lama representatives and marketers to achieve implementation. It includes evidence of decision-making processes, prototype testing, implementation plan and costing, and a detailed final design breakdown.
