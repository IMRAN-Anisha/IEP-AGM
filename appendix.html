00 Glossary & key terms

A quick reference for any
unfamiliar language.

The project crosses engineering, community context, and design vocabulary. Search or filter the glossary below to find any term used across this site.

SAPS Technical +

Stand-Alone Power System. An electrical generation system that operates independently of the main grid. In the Lama Lama context, this typically means solar PV panels paired with battery storage and a charge controller, supplying ranger stations and community facilities at Port Stewart.

PV (Photovoltaic) Technical +

Technology that converts sunlight directly into electricity. Solar PV panels are the primary energy generation source for SAPS installations in remote off-grid communities.

GSM Module Technical +

A cellular communication module that allows the system to send and receive SMS messages over standard mobile networks. Selected for our solution because it functions on minimal bandwidth and does not require internet connectivity.

IP Rating Technical +

Ingress Protection rating. A two-digit code (e.g. IP65) classifying the degree of protection a sealed enclosure provides against dust and water. Higher ratings indicate greater resistance to environmental ingress — critical for tropical, wet-season deployment.

Threshold-based detection Technical +

A fault-detection approach that compares live sensor readings against predefined safe operating limits. If a measurement crosses a threshold (e.g. battery voltage too low, panel temperature too high), the system classifies it as a fault and issues an alert.

Microcontroller Technical +

A small, low-power computer-on-a-chip that runs the firmware controlling the monitoring system. Reads sensor inputs, applies threshold logic, logs events, and triggers alerts via the GSM module.

Lama Lama Country Community +

The lands and waters of the Lama Lama Peoples, located around Port Stewart on Cape York Peninsula, Queensland. The community we are designing in partnership with for this challenge.

Ranger Community +

In this project, Rangers are members of the Lama Lama community who monitor and care for Country, collecting environmental data and managing infrastructure. They are the primary users of the proposed energy-monitoring system.

Country Community +

In Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander cultures, Country refers to far more than land — it encompasses the people, waters, sky, plants, animals, stories, and responsibilities of place. We capitalise the word out of respect.

Acknowledgement of Country Community +

A statement of respect that recognises Traditional Custodians of the land. Distinct from a Welcome to Country, which is delivered by Traditional Owners themselves.

PESTLE Analysis Design Process +

A strategic framework used to scan the broader context affecting a project across six lenses: Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal and Environmental.

User Journey Map Design Process +

A visual representation of a user's experience over time across phases of interaction with a system. Tracks events, emotional states, breakthroughs and pain points to surface design opportunities.

Rainbow Stakeholder Map Design Process +

A semicircular diagram plotting stakeholders along two axes: their level of influence over a project and the degree to which they are affected by it. Used to prioritise engagement and design focus.

Decision Matrix Design Process +

A weighted-scoring tool used to compare concepts against a set of criteria (e.g. cost, durability, usability), helping the team select a direction in a transparent and defensible way.

EWB Challenge Design Process +

An annual design programme run by Engineers Without Borders Australia, in which first-year university students partner with a community to develop human-centred engineering proposals. Team 7 is working on Research Area 3.4: Energy Monitoring and Fault Diagnosis.

Aboriginal Cultural Heritage Act 2003 (QLD) Legal +

Queensland legislation providing recognition, protection and conservation of Aboriginal cultural heritage. Establishes a statutory duty of care on all land users to avoid harming cultural heritage.

Electrical Safety Act 2002 (QLD) Legal +

Establishes legal duties, licensing requirements and safety obligations to prevent electrical hazards in Queensland workplaces and public spaces. All electrical components of the proposed system must comply with this Act.

Work Health and Safety Act 2011 (QLD) Legal +

Provides a comprehensive framework for ensuring the health, safety and welfare of everyone in a workplace, outlining the responsibilities of employers and employees, and establishing safety standards and risk-management provisions.

Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) Legal +

Commonwealth legislation regulating the collection, use, storage and disclosure of personal information by organisations and government agencies. Relevant where the system records usage patterns that could be linked to individuals.
