01 Our Design Solution

Reliable power,
quietly looked after.

Our solution improves the reliability and long-term performance of SAPS by enabling early fault detection, reducing the risk of equipment damage, and minimising system downtime. By providing timely insights into system health, it also supports faster and more effective remote maintenance responses.

This is achieved through continuous monitoring of key operating parameters — current, voltage, temperature and moisture — analysed using threshold-based fault detection methods to identify abnormal conditions. When potential issues are detected, the system automatically sends SMS alerts, allowing operators and maintenance teams to respond quickly before faults escalate.

  • Continuous monitoring Tracks current, voltage, temperature and moisture in real time to provide full visibility into system performance.
  • Threshold-based diagnosis Compares monitored data against predefined safe limits to detect overvoltage, excessive current, overheating, or moisture intrusion.
  • Automatic SMS alerts Notifies operators or maintenance personnel the moment a fault or warning condition is identified — enabling rapid, remote response.
  • Built for resilience Minimises equipment damage, reduces maintenance costs, and strengthens the overall resilience of the energy system in remote community settings.
02 Components Overview

The system, at a glance.

The system is built from a small number of robust, well-understood parts — a sensing layer that watches the panels and batteries, a processing layer that interprets that data, and a communications layer that gets the message out when something is wrong.

S

Sensors

Current, voltage, temperature, and moisture sensors mounted at key points across the SAPS.

M

Microcontroller

A low-power controller reads sensor data, applies threshold logic, and triggers alerts.

G

GSM Module

Sends SMS notifications over cellular networks — no internet required.

E

Enclosure

Weatherproof, UV-stable housing designed for tropical, wildlife-prone environments.

03 Hardware

Designed for heat, humidity
and the long wet season.

Hardware render · placeholder

A rugged sensing layer

Sensors are mounted directly to the solar charge controller, battery terminals and inside the enclosure to capture the full electrical and environmental picture. All wiring is sealed and rated for outdoor tropical conditions.

Components have been selected for long mean-time-between-failure ratings, low power draw, and field-serviceability — so that when something does eventually wear out, a Ranger can swap it without a specialist callout.

Enclosure diagram · placeholder

A weatherproof enclosure

The IP-rated housing keeps moisture, dust and wildlife out, while a ventilated heat-management design keeps internal temperatures within operating range during the dry season.

Mounting points are designed for both wall and pole installation, with a service hatch that can be opened with standard tools — no proprietary fasteners.

04 Software

Quiet, simple, understandable.

The firmware runs locally on the microcontroller and does not rely on cloud infrastructure. Decisions are made on-device using threshold logic, so the system continues to detect faults even when connectivity drops out.

  • On-device logic All threshold checks, fault classification and alert prioritisation run locally — no internet dependency.
  • Plain-language alerts SMS messages describe the problem and the recommended next step using clear, non-technical language.
  • Event logging Every alert and reading is timestamped and stored for later retrieval, building a maintenance history the community can own.
  • Configurable thresholds Safe operating bands can be tuned by trained Rangers as the system matures and seasonal patterns become clearer.
  • Watchdog & self-heal Built-in watchdog timers restart the firmware if it hangs, ensuring the device returns to monitoring without manual intervention.
  • Open & auditable The codebase is open source and documented, so future maintainers — community or otherwise — can read, repair and extend it.
05 Testing

Proof, not promises.

Before deployment, the system is validated against the design requirements set out in Define. Each test maps back to a specific user need — uptime, latency, training time, and recoverability.

01

Environmental

IP-rating verification, UV exposure, thermal cycling and humidity chamber tests.

02

Electrical

Calibration of sensors against reference instruments and fault-injection tests.

03

Usability

Time-to-install and time-to-diagnose trials with non-technical users.

04

Field Pilot

Extended deployment on a representative SAPS, monitoring for false positives and missed events.
